Basic Embroidery Stitches

Before diving into more advanced embroidery techniques, it's important to master the basics. The following embroidery stitches are a great place to start:

Straight Stitch

The straight stitch is one of the most basic embroidery stitches. It involves pushing the needle in and out of the fabric in a straight line. This stitch can be used to outline shapes or add text to your embroidery.

Backstitch

The backstitch is another fundamental embroidery stitch. Like the name suggests, this stitch involves stitching backward, creating a solid line of thread. It's commonly used for outlining designs or creating lettering.

Satin Stitch

The satin stitch is a bit more advanced than the straight and back stitches. It's used to fill in areas with thread, creating a smooth and shiny finish. This stitch can be used to create solid blocks of color or to add texture to your embroidery.
