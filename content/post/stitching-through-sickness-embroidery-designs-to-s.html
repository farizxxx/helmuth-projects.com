Embroidery is more than just a craft, it's a way to spread awareness and hope. That's the idea behind the book Stitching Through Sickness: Embroidery Designs to Spread Awareness and Hope. This book, by Vicki Haninger, contains embroidery designs that are meant to bring light to illnesses and diseases that affect so many people.

Embroidery as Therapy

Embroidery has long been considered a therapeutic activity. It can help with stress and anxiety, as well as providing a sense of accomplishment. The designs in Stitching Through Sickness take that therapy to the next level by creating designs that focus on different illnesses.

Spreading Awareness and Hope

Embroidery is not only therapeutic for the person doing it, but also serves as a way to spread awareness about different illnesses. For example, the book contains designs for breast cancer awareness, HIV/AIDS awareness, and autism awareness. By stitching these designs and wearing them, people can help start conversations and raise awareness about these important issues.

Creating a Community

One of the most powerful things about embroidery is that it can create a sense of community. The designs in Stitching Through Sickness allow people to connect with others who may be going through similar situations. They can also serve as a way to show support for loved ones who are battling illnesses.

In conclusion, Stitching Through Sickness: Embroidery Designs to Spread Awareness and Hope is more than just a book of embroidery designs. It's a way to spread awareness, promote healing, and create a sense of community. By incorporating these designs into our stitching projects, we can help make a difference.
